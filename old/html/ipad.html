A look at the iPad Air 2



Who needs a tablet? Chances are, if you’re viewing this right now, you might be in the market for one.
Why should you care about the Air 2? A tablet is supposed to fill the gap between a smartphone and a laptop, right?
The iPad air 2 is Apple’s latest attempt at trying to do that very thing.

What first impresses me when looking at a new product is it’s design.
If you’re familiar with any of Apple’s other products, you won’t be surprised that it looks stunning, I mean, just look at it!



What sets this apart from the rest of the competition? It’s the most powerful tablet you’re able to purchase at this very moment (At the time this was written).
What does that mean for you? You’re getting a very fluid experience using this tablet.
As the name suggests, you’re getting a very thin and light tablet, which is a necessity if you’re going to be using this iPad for any length of time.
This tablet is using Apple’s A8X chip. It performs incredibly well, with this Apple was able to optimize iOS to be as energy efficient as possible.

This means one thing: amazing battery life. I can easily not charge this iPad for weeks at a time, while getting some use out of it.
It even has an incredibly sharp display, coming in at a resolution of 2048×1536. If you’re asking about the camera, stop.
Seriously, chances are if you have this iPad, or are thinking of getting it, you have a smartphone to accompany it.
Don’t use this for pictures (You’re doing us all a favor).

As I mentioned before, this tablet is running iOS 9.
This means that the Air 2 has some extremely useful features such as split-screen multitasking and Siri has been improved tremendously.
This is what multitasking on the Air 2 looks like:



Let me be the first to tell you that this is one very welcome feature. It truly helps with getting stuff done on the iPad.
After all, a tablet should be bridging the gap between a smartphone and a laptop, right?
It also helps bridge that gap if you spring for more storage, instead of 16gb base configuration Apple offers.

Let’s talk about one thing that will be the ultimate factor on whether or not you’ll be purchasing this tablet, price.
Starting at 549$ CAN and 420$ USD, this tablet is fairly expensive. Chances are you’ll want to upgrade the storage which will cost even more.

Is it worth it? That’s up to you. If it were me, I’d choose this iPad over any other tablet time and time again.
